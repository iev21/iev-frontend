Introduction to Battery Modeling

Course Description:

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the modeling of battery systems for battery electric vehicles (BEVs). Students will learn the fundamental principles of battery chemistry, the dynamics of battery operation, and the various modeling techniques used to simulate battery behavior. The course includes both theoretical and practical components, with hands-on exercises in battery modeling and simulation.

Course Objectives:

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand the basic principles of battery chemistry and operation.
  • Understand mathematical models to describe battery behavior.
  • Learn how to use simulation tools for battery modelling and analysis.
  • Interpret simulation results to make informed decisions about battery design and usage.
  • Recognize current trends and future directions in battery modeling and simulation.

Course Outline:

Module 1: Introduction to Battery Electric Vehicles

  • Overview of battery electric vehicles
  • Importance of battery systems in BEVs
  • Basic components of a battery system

Module 2: Fundamentals of Battery Chemistry

  • Basic electrochemistry
  • Types of battery chemistries
  • Working principles of lithium-ion batteries
  • Key performance metrics

Module 3: Battery Modeling Principles

  • Introduction to battery modeling
  • Types of battery models: empirical, electrochemical, and equivalent circuit models
  • Overview of modeling tools and software

Module 4: Empirical Battery Models

  • Overview of empirical models
  • Data-driven modeling approaches
  • Fitting models to experimental data
  • Applications and limitations

Module 5: Electrochemical Battery Models

  • Overview of electrochemical models
  • Governing equations: Nernst-Planck, Butler-Volmer, and Poisson equations
  • Modeling battery charge and discharge cycles
  • Applications and limitations

Module 6: Equivalent Circuit Battery Models

  • Overview of equivalent circuit models
  • RC (resistor-capacitor) circuits
  • Modeling transient and steady-state behavior
  • Applications and limitations

Module 7: Integration with Vehicle Powertrains

  • Role of batteries in BEV powertrains
  • Coupling battery models with powertrain models
  • Energy management strategies
  • Simulation of vehicle-level performance

Module 8: Future Trends and Innovations

  • Advancements in battery materials and design
  • Innovations in modeling techniques
  • The role of AI and machine learning in battery modeling
  • Future directions and market trends

Assessment Methods

  • Quizzes: Weekly quizzes to assess understanding of core concepts.
  • Assignments: Periodic assignments including readings, discussions, and practical activities.
  • Participation: Active participation in discussions and activities.

Additional Resources:

  • "Battery Systems Engineering" by Christopher D. Rahn and Chao-Yang Wang
  • "Battery Management Systems: Design by Modelling" by H.J. Bergveld, W.S. Kruijt, and P.H.L. Notten
  • "Fundamentals of Electrochemical Science" by Keith Oldham and Jan Myland
  • Relevant academic papers and industry reports
