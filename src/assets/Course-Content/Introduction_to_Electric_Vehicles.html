Introduction to Electric Vehicles

This course provides an introductory overview of electric vehicles (EVs), focusing on their history, technology, and impact on society and the environment. It aims to equip students with a foundational understanding of EV components, functionality, and the factors driving the transition from traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles to electric mobility. The course covers technological advancements, market trends, policy frameworks, and the environmental and economic implications of electric vehicles.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the historical development and evolution of electric vehicles.
  • Identify and explain the main components of electric vehicles and their functions.
  • Analyze the benefits and challenges associated with electric vehicles.
  • Evaluate the environmental and economic impacts of electric vehicle adoption.
  • Understand current market trends and policies influencing the EV industry.
  • Explore future advancements and innovations in electric vehicle technology.

Course Outline

Module 1: Introduction and History of Electric Vehicles

  • Lecture: Overview of the Course and Introduction to Electric Vehicles
  • Reading: History and Evolution of Electric Vehicles
  • Discussion: The Rise and Fall of Early Electric Vehicles
  • Activity: Timeline Creation of Key Milestones in EV Development

Module 2: Basic Principles of Electric Vehicles

  • Lecture: Fundamental Concepts of Electricity and Electric Motors
  • Reading: Understanding Electric Power and Energy Storage
  • Discussion: Comparing Electric Motors and Internal Combustion Engines
  • Activity: Simple Electric Motor Construction

Module 3: EV Components and Architecture

  • Lecture: Key Components of Electric Vehicles: Battery, Motor, and Controller
  • Reading: Types of Batteries Used in EVs
  • Discussion: The Role of Power Electronics in EVs
  • Activity: Virtual Tour of EV Assembly Plant

Module 4: Battery Technology

  • Lecture: Battery Technologies: Types, Charging, and Management
  • Reading: Lithium-Ion vs. Solid-State Batteries
  • Discussion: Challenges in Battery Development
  • Activity: Battery Performance Analysis Lab

Module 5: Charging Infrastructure

  • Lecture: Types of Charging: AC vs. DC, Levels of Charging
  • Reading: Infrastructure for Electric Vehicles: Public and Private Solutions
  • Discussion: The Role of Charging Infrastructure in EV Adoption
  • Activity: Designing a Charging Network for a City

Module 6: Environmental and Economic Impacts

  • Lecture: Environmental Benefits of Electric Vehicles
  • Reading: Economic Analysis of EVs vs. ICE Vehicles
  • Discussion: Lifecycle Emissions of EVs
  • Activity: Cost-Benefit Analysis of Switching to EVs

Module 7: Policy and Market Trends

  • Lecture: Global Policies and Incentives for Electric Vehicles
  • Reading: Current Market Trends and Future Projections
  • Discussion: Policy Case Studies from Different Countries
  • Activity: Debate on EV Policies and Their Effectiveness

Module 8: Future of Electric Vehicles

  • Lecture: Emerging Technologies and Innovations in EVs
  • Reading: Autonomous and Connected EVs
  • Discussion: The Future of Urban Mobility with Electric Vehicles
  • Activity: Group Project on Designing the Next Generation EV

Assessment Methods

  • Quizzes: Weekly quizzes to assess understanding of core concepts.
  • Assignments: Periodic assignments including readings, discussions, and practical activities.
  • Participation: Active participation in discussions and activities.

Recommended Reading

  • "Electric Vehicle Engineering" by James Larminie and John Lowry
  • "Electric Vehicles: The Future of Road Transportation" by Peter Harrop
  • "Sustainable Transportation and the Electric Vehicle Revolution" by Tony Seba
