Introduction to Battery Electric Vehicle Performance Analysis

Course Description:

This course provides an in-depth introduction to the performance analysis of battery electric vehicles (BEVs). Students will learn the fundamental principles of BEV performance metrics, the tools and techniques used to analyze vehicle performance, and the key factors influencing the efficiency and effectiveness of BEVs. The course covers both theoretical concepts and practical applications through hands-on exercises and case studies.

Course Objectives:

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand the key performance metrics for BEVs.
  • Analyze the performance of BEV powertrains and battery systems.
  • Understand the importance of simulation tools to evaluate BEV performance.
  • Identify and optimize factors affecting BEV efficiency.
  • Interpret performance data to make informed decisions about BEV design and operation.
  • Recognize current trends and future directions in BEV performance analysis.

Course Outline:

Module 1: Introduction to Battery Electric Vehicles

  • Overview of BEVs
  • Importance of performance analysis in BEV development
  • Basic components of a BEV

Module 2: Key Performance Metrics

  • Energy efficiency
  • Range and energy consumption
  • Power and acceleration
  • Regenerative braking efficiency

Module 3: Fundamentals of Vehicle Dynamics

  • Basic principles of vehicle dynamics
  • Longitudinal dynamics: acceleration, deceleration, and braking
  • Lateral dynamics: handling and stability
  • Impact of aerodynamics and rolling resistance

Module 4: Battery Performance Analysis

  • Battery capacity and energy density
  • State of Charge (SOC) and State of Health (SOH)
  • Battery thermal management
  • Cycle life and degradation

Module 5: Powertrain Performance Analysis

  • Electric motor performance characteristics
  • Inverter and power electronics efficiency
  • Transmission and drivetrain efficiency
  • Overall powertrain efficiency

Module 6: Energy Consumption and Range Analysis

  • Factors affecting energy consumption
  • Driving cycles and real-world driving conditions
  • Range estimation techniques
  • Impact of auxiliary systems on range

Module 7: Regenerative Braking Analysis

  • Principles of regenerative braking
  • Efficiency of energy recovery
  • Impact on vehicle range and battery life
  • Simulation of regenerative braking systems

Module 8: Simulation Tools and Techniques

  • Overview of simulation software (e.g., MATLAB/Simulink, AVL Cruise)
  • Setting up and running performance simulations
  • Interpreting simulation results
  • Case studies and practical examples

Module 9: Performance Optimization

  • Strategies for optimizing BEV performance
  • Design optimization techniques
  • Energy management strategies
  • Trade-offs and decision-making in BEV design

Module 10: Case Studies and Real-World Applications

  • Analysis of existing BEV models
  • Comparison of different BEV architectures
  • Lessons learned from real-world performance data
  • Future directions and industry trends

Module 11: Future Trends and Innovations

  • Advancements in BEV technology
  • Role of AI and machine learning in performance analysis
  • Future directions in BEV performance optimization
  • Market trends and consumer expectations

Assessment Methods

  • Quizzes: Weekly quizzes to assess understanding of core concepts.
  • Assignments: Periodic assignments including readings, discussions, and practical activities.
  • Participation: Active participation in discussions and activities.

Additional Resources:

  • "Electric Vehicle Technology Explained" by James Larminie and John Lowry
  • "Fundamentals of Vehicle Dynamics" by Thomas D. Gillespie
  • "Automotive Powertrain Science and Technology" by Fredrik Westin
  • Relevant academic papers and industry reports
