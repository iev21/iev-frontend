Intermediate Battery Electric Vehicle Powertrain Simulation Course

Course Description:

This intermediate-level course focuses on the simulation and analysis of battery electric vehicle (BEV) powertrains. Students will deepen their understanding of powertrain components, simulation techniques, and performance optimization strategies. The course includes practical exercises using advanced simulation tools to model, analyze, and optimize BEV powertrains.

Course Objectives:

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand advanced concepts in BEV powertrain design and simulation.
  • Develop and analyze detailed models of BEV powertrain components.
  • Use simulation tools to evaluate and optimize BEV performance.
  • Interpret simulation results to make informed decisions about powertrain design and optimization.
  • Recognize current trends and future directions in BEV powertrain simulation.

Course Outline:

Module 1: Review of Basic BEV Powertrain Concepts

  • Overview of BEV powertrain components
  • Basic principles of powertrain operation
  • Introduction to powertrain simulation tools

Module 2: Detailed Modeling of Electric Motors

  • Electric motor types and characteristics
  • Mathematical modeling of electric motors
  • Simulation of motor performance under different conditions

Module 3: Battery Modeling and Simulation

  • Advanced battery modeling techniques
  • State of Charge (SOC) and State of Health (SOH) estimation
  • Thermal modeling of batteries
  • Simulation of battery performance and degradation

Module 4: Power Electronics and Control Systems

  • Overview of power electronics in BEVs
  • Modeling of inverters and converters
  • Control strategies for power electronics
  • Simulation of power electronics performance

Module 5: Drivetrain and Transmission Modeling

  • Types of drivetrains and their configurations
  • Modeling of transmission systems
  • Simulation of drivetrain dynamics
  • Efficiency analysis and optimization

Module 6: Vehicle Dynamics and Performance Simulation

  • Introduction to vehicle dynamics
  • Modeling of longitudinal and lateral dynamics
  • Simulation of vehicle handling and stability
  • Impact of vehicle dynamics on powertrain performance

Module 7: Energy Management Strategies

  • Importance of energy management in BEVs
  • Optimization of energy usage
  • Regenerative braking and energy recovery
  • Simulation of energy management strategies

Module 8: Thermal Management of Powertrains

  • Thermal challenges in BEV powertrains
  • Cooling and heating systems for batteries and motors
  • Thermal modeling and simulation
  • Impact of thermal management on performance and safety

Module 9: Simulation Tools and Techniques

  • Overview of advanced simulation software
  • Understanding detailed powertrain simulations
  • Interpreting and analyzing simulation results
  • Case studies and practical applications

Module 10: Performance Optimization and Trade-offs

  • Strategies for optimizing BEV performance
  • Design optimization techniques
  • Trade-offs between performance, efficiency, and cost
  • Case studies of optimized powertrain designs

Assessment:

  • Quizzes and assignments to reinforce learning
  • Assignments: Periodic assignments including readings, discussions, and practical activities.
  • Participation: Active participation in discussions and activities.
  • Final project/presentation on a specific BEV powertrain simulation topic

Recommended Textbooks and Resources:

  • "Electric Vehicle Technology Explained" by James Larminie and John Lowry
  • "Fundamentals of Vehicle Dynamics" by Thomas D. Gillespie
  • "Automotive Powertrain Science and Technology" by Fredrik Westin
  • "Hybrid Electric Vehicles: Principles and Applications with Practical Perspectives" by Chris Mi, M. Abul Masrur, and David Wenzhong Gao
  • "Automotive Powertrain Simulation and Control: Model, Methods and Applications" by Michael Plint and Tony Martyr
  • Relevant academic papers and industry reports
