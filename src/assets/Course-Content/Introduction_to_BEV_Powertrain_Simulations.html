Introduction to Battery Electric Vehicle Powertrain Simulations

Course Description:

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the simulation of battery electric vehicle (BEV) powertrains. Students will learn the fundamental principles of powertrain components, how to model and simulate these components, and the tools and techniques used in the industry to analyze and optimize BEV performance. The course covers both theoretical aspects and practical applications through hands-on calculation exercises.

Course Objectives:

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand the basic principles of BEV powertrains and their components.
  • How to Model and simulate the behavior of BEV powertrain components.
  • Use simulation tools to analyze and optimize BEV performance.
  • Interpret simulation results to make informed design decisions.
  • Recognize current trends and future directions in BEV powertrain simulation.

Course Outline:

Module 1: Introduction to Battery Electric Vehicles

  • Overview of electric vehicles
  • Components of BEV powertrains
  • Importance of simulation in vehicle development

Module 2: Fundamentals of Powertrain Components

  • Battery systems
  • Electric motors and controllers
  • Transmission and drivetrain
  • Auxiliary systems

Module 3: Introduction to Simulation Tools

  • Overview of simulation software (e.g., MATLAB/Simulink, ANSYS, GT-SUITE)
  • How to Set up simulation environments
  • Basic modeling techniques

Module 4: Battery Modeling and Simulation Concepts

  • Battery chemistry and dynamics
  • State-of-charge (SOC) estimation
  • Thermal modeling of batteries
  • Simulation of battery performance

Module 5: Electric Motor Modeling and Simulation Concepts

  • Types of electric motors used in BEVs
  • Mathematical modeling of motors
  • Motor control strategies
  • Simulation of motor performance

Module 6: Power Electronics and Control Systems

  • Inverters and converters
  • Control algorithms for power electronics
  • Integration with motor and battery systems
  • Simulation of control strategies

Module 7: Drivetrain and Vehicle Dynamics Simulation

  • Drivetrain components and their models
  • Vehicle dynamics principles
  • Longitudinal and lateral dynamics
  • Simulation of vehicle performance

Module 8: Energy Management Strategies

  • Importance of energy management
  • Optimization of energy usage
  • Modeling energy management systems
  • Simulation of energy management strategies

Module 9: Thermal Management in BEVs

  • Thermal challenges in BEVs
  • Cooling and heating systems
  • Thermal modeling and simulation
  • Impact on vehicle performance

Module 10: Analysis and Optimization of Simulation Results

  • Interpreting simulation data
  • Performance metrics and criteria
  • Optimization techniques
  • Case studies and practical examples

Module 11: Future Trends in BEV Powertrain Simulations

  • Advancements in simulation tools and techniques
  • Integration of AI and machine learning
  • Future directions in BEV development
  • Industry trends and market outlook

Assessment Methods

  • Quizzes: Weekly quizzes to assess understanding of core concepts.
  • Assignments: Periodic assignments including readings, discussions, and practical activities.
  • Participation: Active participation in discussions and activities.

  • Relevant academic papers and industry reports
